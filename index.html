Quiet Contemplation

A quiet portrait of my grandmother in her reading chair. Her hands tell the story of a hundred winter mornings.

To look at a thing until it yields its story. That is what photography is to me. It isn't about chasing the grand spectacle, but about finding the holy in the mundane.

Photographer's Philosophy

A Human Approach

I believe in slow photography. I work with natural light, organic interactions, and long pauses. There is a deep, comforting beauty in objects and places that show wear—creased book pages, worn wooden spoons, weathered gates. They invite us to slow down and remember.

This website serves as my digital shelf, a quiet place to stack memories and sketches.
